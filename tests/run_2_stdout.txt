- The minimum cover requirements for direct exposure to salt water and coastal situations of the LRFD-BDS shall apply.  
- Concrete exposed to salt/sea water can be an aggressive corrosive environment; in general, continuous submersion is the least aggressive exposure, while areas with capillary suction and evaporation are the most aggressive.  
- Corrosive water or soil contains greater or equal to 500 part per million (ppm) of chlorides.

- For cast-in-place bridge decks with two layers of reinforcement, a minimum 2½″ of concrete cover over top bars of deck reinforcing shall be provided, including a ½″ wearing surface and ¼″ tolerance for placement.  
- Bottom cover shall be 1” minimum.

- A terminal is used on the approach end and a transition is needed at the bridge end when a guardrail on the approach to a bridge is to be shifted laterally to connect with the bridge rail. Curves (bends) are shown in the guardrail to shift it to the bridge rail. However, the length of the curves are not critical. For new BT installations, use the Buried Terminal Type 2. Previously, another BT option (the Buried Terminal Type 1) was an available choice. For existing installations, it is acceptable to leave this option in service as long as height requirements and other design criteria are met. The backslope needed to install a BT is to be 3H:1V or steeper and at least 4 feet in height above the roadway. Do not place a mound of soil or base material over the Type 2 anchor located at the end of the buried terminal system. Install Type 2 anchors into the backslope.
